Why Now & Moat

Strong tailwinds. High defensibility.

Why Now

AI works. But lives outside core systems.

AI is sold to clinics as point solutions (e.g. scribing, voice agents). Value is lost because it isn't embedded in core systems where operations and decisions happen.

Top-tier capital is investing in this category

Ease Health raised $41M from a16z (AI-native OS for behavioral health). YC-backed Sully.ai raised $20M for AI agents in healthcare. No equivalent in European private clinic ops. The wedge is open.

EHDS forces open the closed-EMR moat

The European Health Data Space regulation (adopted 2025, phased rollout 2026–2027) mandates standardised clinical data formats and APIs across EU member states. Legacy EMRs that locked clinics in via proprietary data formats lose that lock. For the first time, a clinic can switch its operating software without losing its data history.

Incumbents can't ship AI in time

Legacy EMRs were built pre-LLM and pre-API. Bolting on agentic AI means rethinking the entire stack. They lack the AI talent and move slowly by design.

Our Moat
Gokul Rajaram's framework

Workflow moat

Amiro learns each clinic's flows, escalation rules, and SLAs. These fingerprints compound into a workflow knowledge graph across specialties.

Data moat

Patient and clinical data lives behind closed EMRs and is heavily regulated — competitors can't scrape it, buy it, or generate it synthetically. Every clinic Amiro signs adds a proprietary dataset that only Amiro can build on. The longer a clinic runs, the deeper the asset.

Network effects

What one clinic learns, every clinic benefits from. Reactivation campaigns, scheduling patterns, and conversion playbooks are distilled across the network. Clinic #100 inherits the intelligence of the 99 before it.

High switching costs

Amiro touches every role — front desk, clinicians, ops, finance, marketing. Switching isn't a migration; it's a re-org.
